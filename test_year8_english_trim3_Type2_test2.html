THE TEXT

(1) John Preddy, a remarkable young teenager from London, decided to climb Mt. Kilimanjaro – the highest mountain in Africa – as a way to raise money for "Live In Peace".

(2) "When I told my peers that I was thinking about climbing Mt. Kilimanjaro in Tanzania for the organization, they were very excited and amazed," John said. "My dream is to help all the kids from the conflict areas."

(3) With the support of his friends and family, the 15-year-old boy from London was able to raise over $10,000 for the organization. The majority of his support came online via a personalized fundraising site which he created. On the site, supporters sent him messages that kept him "motivated all the way."

(4) The fundraisers help support the charity and spread knowledge about "Live In Peace", which is more important than the actual amount of money raised. In the past, others have raised funds for the same organization but John is the first one to climb a mountain for peace.

(5) "People are able to make peace," John said, "and it's important for young people to take part in peace-making because we will be the next generation that will take on the world's conflicts. If people get this message then it will be easier to solve all the problems. A great example of the possibility of peace is that while I was in Africa, I learnt that Tanzania consists of more than 120 tribes who live together peacefully."

Adapted from http://www.seedsofpeace.org

III- Writing (6 marks)

Sample paragraph:

Michael Jackson was born in 1958 in Indiana. He became one of the most famous singers and dancers in the world. Throughout his career, he used his music to promote peace, tolerance and to support needy children. In 1992 he founded the “Heal the World Foundation”, an organization that aimed to help children threatened by war, poverty and disease. He also donated the funds from many of his concerts to charities. His generosity and activism touched millions of lives. Michael Jackson died in 2009 in California, but his musical legacy and humanitarian work continue to inspire people everywhere.
