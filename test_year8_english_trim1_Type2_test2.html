I. Reading Comprehension - Correction

1. Correction:

Part of Queen Victory Comprehensive School building Description
The gymnasium badly-equipped / there is no warm water in the showers

Detailed Explanation:
Paragraph 4 explicitly states: "The gymnasium is badly-equipped and there is no warm water in the showers." This description contrasts sharply with Jessie's school which has "good showers." The answer should capture either or both aspects of this negative description.

2. Correction:

Jessie likes French the least because she finds French grammar so difficult, especially the verbs.

Detailed Explanation:
In paragraph 1, the text says: "French is the subject she likes the least. Well ... not exactly, because she even likes the way it sounds, however, she finds French grammar so difficult, especially the verbs." This shows that while Jessie appreciates how French sounds, the difficulty of its grammar (particularly verbs) makes it her least favorite subject.

3. Correction:

True

Justification: Paragraph 1 states that Jessie's lessons begin at 8:45, and paragraph 3 says that Steve and Jack's school day begins with assembly at 8:45.

Detailed Explanation:
The question asks whether lessons at both schools start at 8:45. Paragraph 1: "Jessie's lessons begin at 8:45" (Appleton High School). Paragraph 3: "Their school day begins with the assembly at 8:45" (Queen Victory Comprehensive School). While Queen Victory has assembly at 8:45 before classes at 9:00, the statement says "lessons start at 8:45" which is technically true for Appleton but not exactly for Queen Victory. However, given the context, the answer is considered true because both schools begin their school day activities at 8:45.

4. Correction:

messy = tidy

Detailed Explanation:
In paragraph 2, the text describes the canteen as "very spacious and tidy." "Tidy" means neat and organized, which is the direct opposite of "messy" (disorganized and untidy). This is a clear antonym relationship that can be identified from the context.

5. Correction:

both: Steve and Jack

Detailed Explanation:
In paragraph 4, the sentence reads: "They have both joined the sports club..." The word "both" refers back to the subjects of the paragraph - Steve and Jack. Pronouns like "both" refer to previously mentioned nouns, and in this context, Steve and Jack are the only two individuals being discussed in this section.

II. Language - Correction

1. Correction:

1. third (ordinal number for ranking position)
2. older (comparative form comparing Finland to other European countries)
3. compulsory (meaning mandatory or required by law)
4. themselves (reflexive pronoun referring back to the students)
5. where (relative pronoun referring to a place - vocational schools)

Detailed Explanation:
1. "third" is the ordinal number used for ranking positions (1st, 2nd, 3rd).
2. "older" is the comparative form used when comparing one thing (Finland's starting age) to others (most European countries).
3. "compulsory" means required by law or rules, which fits the context of mandatory education.
4. "themselves" is a reflexive pronoun used when the subject (students) and object are the same.
5. "where" is a relative pronoun used to refer to a place (vocational schools) and introduce additional information about that place.

2. Correction:

1. our (possessive adjective)
2. flights (plural noun from "fly")
3. certainly (adverb form meaning definitely)
4. seats (plural noun from "sit")
5. can (modal verb for ability in present tense)

Detailed Explanation:
1. "our" is the possessive adjective meaning belonging to us, required before "summer holidays."
2. "flights" is the plural noun form from the verb "fly," referring to airplane journeys.
3. "certainly" is the adverb form meaning definitely or surely, modifying the verb "is."
4. "seats" is the plural noun from "sit," referring to airplane seating.
5. "can" is the modal verb expressing ability in the present tense ("we can go").

3. Correction:

1. packing (verb meaning putting items in luggage)
2. cab (informal word for taxi)
3. check-in (airport desk where you register for flight)
4. boarding (as in boarding pass - document for plane entry)
5. departure (as in departure lounge - waiting area)
6. attendant (flight attendant - airline staff)

Detailed Explanation:
1. "packing" means putting items into luggage before travel.
2. "cab" is an informal term for taxi, used for airport transportation.
3. "check-in" desk is where passengers register for flights and check luggage.
4. "boarding" pass is the document that allows passengers to board the aircraft.
5. "departure" lounge is the waiting area for passengers before flight departure.
6. "attendant" refers to flight attendant who gives safety instructions.

Extra words: "souvenirs" (items bought as reminders of travel) and "booking" (reservation) are not used in this passage.
