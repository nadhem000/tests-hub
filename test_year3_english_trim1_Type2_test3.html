Many years ago, Jeff, now 59, had a serious accident at work that left him with permanent injuries. He's now a volunteer with Sporting Chance, a project that engages older men in the north east in activity and exercise groups, and runs regular bingo sessions.

The change that Jeff has experienced in his life as a result of volunteering is something many of the 30,000 involved with our Volunteering Matters charity have also seen. There is now broad agreement about the positive contribution volunteering can have for people's health and wellbeing. Volunteering helps reduce loneliness, now recognised as a serious health risk, and is one of the community-led approaches that can help improve mental health. Volunteering can help to provide people with ways out of poverty, by giving them new skills and confidence, and aid social integration.

But while the benefits of volunteering are clear, there is worrying evidence that the people who could benefit most from giving their time are precisely those least likely to be involved. There are many reasons for people not to volunteer. For older people, the barriers can include poor health, poverty, lack of skills, poor transport links, or having caring responsibilities, such as looking after grandchildren. Younger people can be deterred by feeling they don't have the time to volunteer, or not knowing anyone else who volunteers. Many also think volunteering is just for older people with time on their hands.

Detailed Explanation:
The information is directly extracted from paragraph 1: "He's now a volunteer with Sporting Chance, a project that engages older men in the north east in activity and exercise groups, and runs regular bingo sessions." The project name is "Sporting Chance" and it has two aims: 1) engaging older men in activity/exercise groups, and 2) running regular bingo sessions. Both aims must be mentioned to get full marks.

3. Correction:

a. False statement correction:

"There is now broad agreement about the positive contribution volunteering can have"

Explanation: The statement says "only a few people think" but the text says "broad agreement," which means most people agree. "Broad agreement" indicates widespread or general agreement, contradicting "only a few people."

b. False statement correction:

"Volunteering can help to provide people with ways out of poverty, by giving them new skills and confidence"

Explanation: The statement says volunteering allows people to "get enriched" (meaning become wealthy), but the text says it helps provide "ways out of poverty" through skills and confidence, not necessarily making them rich. There's a difference between escaping poverty and becoming enriched/wealthy.

c. False statement correction:

"Younger people can be deterred by feeling they don't have the time to volunteer, or not knowing anyone else who volunteers"

Explanation: The statement claims young people don't volunteer because they think it's "hard work," but the text gives different reasons: lack of time and not knowing other volunteers. The text doesn't mention "hard work" as a reason, making the statement false.

4. Correction:

a. "They" refers to:

The 30,000 people involved with the Volunteering Matters charity

b. "Their" refers to:

The people who could benefit most from volunteering

Detailed Explanation:
a. In paragraph 2, "They" refers back to "the 30,000 involved with our Volunteering Matters charity" mentioned earlier in the same sentence.
b. In paragraph 3, "their time" refers to "the people who could benefit most from giving their time" mentioned at the beginning of the paragraph. Pronouns typically refer to the nearest preceding noun or noun phrase.

deterred

Detailed Explanation:
In paragraph 3, the sentence reads: "Younger people can be deterred by feeling they don't have the time to volunteer..." The word "deterred" means discouraged or prevented from doing something. Both words convey the idea of being put off or prevented from taking action due to certain factors.

4. "death" - Noun form required (die ‚Üí death)
5. "was" - Past simple passive voice (she was awarded)
6. "has shaped" - Present perfect tense indicating action from past to present
