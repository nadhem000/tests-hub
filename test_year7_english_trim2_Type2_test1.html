Text: A surprise party

1. On Friday night my husband David and I went to a surprise fiftieth birthday party for our friend Therese. Her friend Janet arranged the party a few weeks ago, but Therese knew nothing about it.

2. We all met at the restaurant at a quarter to seven. There were about 50 people. We had a separate room at the back. Therese's son asked Therese to come to the restaurant at 7 o'clock. Just before seven we all stood in one corner of the room and the lights were turned off. It wasn't completely dark, but the room looked empty from the door.

3. Therese's son went to meet her and brought her to the room. He stood back politely so she could go in first. And that's when we all shouted, 'Surprise!'

4. Therese was very surprised indeed. She jumped when she heard the voices. Then we sang Happy Birthday and she understood what was happening. We all had a delicious meal followed by a slice of chocolate birthday cake.

5. For her birthday, Therese got a beautiful dress. There were lots of squares and rectangles of colour. Some of the rectangles were family photos printed onto material, so it's a very special family present. Therese enjoyed her evening despite the shock when she first arrived.

III- Writing (6 marks)

Task : Read Michael's letter to Tom. Then write back to him. Advise him and help him solve his problem of being overweight. (no more than twelve lines)

Michael's letter:

Dear Tom,
How are you? I'm writing to tell you that I'm so sad. All my schoolmates make fun of me because of my weight. They call me fat boy. I know this is true but what can I do? I love eating chocolate cakes, fast food, spaghetti and drinking much cola! Can you help me? What should I do? Write back soon.
Yours, Michael

Your reply (to Michael):

Assessment: Task achievement /2 ; Lexis & grammar /2 ; Mechanics /2.

Detailed correction

I- Reading Comprehension

1- c) A surprise party (best title).

2- When: Friday night / What: went to a surprise birthday party for their friend Therese.

3- a) False. The guests arrived at a quarter to seven at the restaurant, not at the writer's house. (Text: "We all met at the restaurant at a quarter to seven.") b) False. She was shocked but she was happy: "Therese was very surprised indeed. She jumped ... Then we sang Happy Birthday and she understood ... enjoyed her evening".

4- b) seemed ("looked" here means "appeared").

5- A beautiful dress with family photos printed on it.

6- "It" refers to the room. / "He" refers to Therese's son.

II- Language

Exercise 1: woke up, Her, looked, present (from list), have given, nose, scarf, head. (Extra words: heart, trousers)

Exercise 2: 1→e (asking for help), 2→c (making a request), 3→a (asking for information), 4→b (making a suggestion). (d) thanking is extra.

Exercise 3: permission, me, thought, on.

III- Writing - Planning & sample

Planning (advice letter structure)

  • Greeting and sympathy: Thank Michael for his letter, express understanding.
  • Identify the problem: Acknowledge his feelings and the situation.
  • Give practical advice: Suggest healthy eating (less fast food, more fruits/vegetables), drinking water instead of cola, doing sports or activities.
  • Encouragement: Remind him that he can change, and that true friends don't mock.
  • Closing: Offer further support, sign off.

Sample reply (approx. 15 lines)

Dear Michael,

I was sorry to hear that your schoolmates make fun of you. It's not right, but don't lose hope – you can improve your health and feel better!

First, try to change your eating habits. Instead of chocolate cakes and fast food, eat more fruits, vegetables, and grilled chicken. Drink water instead of cola – it's healthier and has no sugar. Also, start moving! You can join a sports club, ride a bike, or simply walk every day. Exercise helps burn calories and makes you stronger.

Remember, small steps lead to big changes. Don't give up! If you need motivation, I can be your partner. Write to me anytime.

Best wishes,
Tom

(Note: The sample is longer than 12 lines to show detail; students should write concisely.)
