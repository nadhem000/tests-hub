Text: Where to go for spring holidays?

Mrs Soltane: Where do you think we should go for our spring holidays?

Mariem: How far is it from here?

Mr Soltane: It's about 400 kilometers.

Mariem: But that's too far and the weather is windy and cold.

Mr Soltane: You are right. Let's think of a closer place.

Mrs Soltane: What do you think of Hammamet?

Yahya: Hammamet! Yeah! Hammamet is good. Let's go there this year.

Mr Soltane: It's ok with me. What about you Mariem?

Mariem: It's a good idea. Hammamet is the place then.

Mr Soltane: But before that I must book our rooms in a hotel.

Mrs Soltane: Don't book expensive ones, please. We will spend most of our time in the sea or outside the hotel.

Mr Soltane: You are right. Let me call the hotel receptionist then.

III- Writing (6 marks)

Task: Write a seven-line article in your school magazine in which you talk about what you do in your spare time when the weather is sunny and hot.

📝 Detailed correction

I- Reading Comprehension

1- ✅ a) Hammamet. (They finally agree on Hammamet).

2- a) 4 participants (Mrs Soltane, Yahya, Mr Soltane, Mariem). b) Because he likes the south very much and the food is great.

3- ❌ False. Mariem says: “But that's too far and the weather is windy and cold.” She does NOT want to go to Gabes.

4- ✅ b) Asking about distance.

5- “book” (line: “I must book our rooms”).

II- Language

Task1: 1. capital, 2. bus, 3. means, 4. cheap, 5. booked, 6. where.

Task2: traffic / get / rush / dirty / faster / more expensive.

Task3: 1 → d (Two times a week), 2 → a (It's only 10 meters), 3 → c (5 minutes), 4 → b (It's 20 $).

III- Writing – Planning & model (30‑line article)

📋 Planning (narrative descriptive)

  • Introduction: mention the season (sunny, hot) and general feeling.
  • Outdoor activities: swimming at the beach, picnics in the park, playing football.
  • Indoor alternatives: reading under a fan, drinking cold lemonade, watching movies.
  • Evening time: walks, ice cream with friends.
  • Conclusion: why you enjoy sunny days.

🌴 30‑line model article

My Sunny Spare Time When the weather is sunny and hot, I feel full of energy and happiness. The bright sun invites me to leave my room and enjoy the outdoors. In my spare time, I try to make the most of every warm moment. Usually, I start my day with a quick breakfast and then head to the beach with my friends. The water is cool and refreshing, so we swim and splash for hours. We also build sandcastles and collect shiny shells. Sometimes we play beach volleyball, and the sand burns our feet — but we love it! After the beach, we often go to a nearby park. There are big trees that give us shade. We bring a blanket, some sandwiches, and a big bottle of homemade lemonade. Lying on the grass, we chat, laugh, and watch the clouds. The gentle breeze makes the heat bearable. On extremely hot afternoons, I prefer indoor activities. I stay in my air‑conditioned room, read an adventure novel, or watch funny videos. My mother prepares cold watermelon or mango juice — it’s delicious! Sometimes I help her make popsicles with real fruit. As the sun begins to set, the temperature drops a little. That’s my favourite time to go for a bike ride around the neighbourhood. The sky turns orange and pink, and I feel free. I often meet neighbours and we talk about our day. In the evening, my family and I go for a walk to the ice‑cream parlour. I always order a double scoop of chocolate chip. We sit outside, listen to the crickets, and enjoy the last rays of light. Those moments are simple but precious. On weekends, we might have a barbecue in the garden. My father grills chicken and corn, and we eat outside under the stars. It’s a wonderful way to end a hot day. To sum up, sunny and hot weather gives me endless possibilities to relax, have fun, and connect with people I love. I never feel bored because there is always something enjoyable to do — whether at the beach, at home, or in the park. I truly cherish these moments and I’m already looking forward to the next sunny afternoon.
