Test Correction

Part I: Language - Correction

1/ Correction:

Possible Answers: 1. does, 2. laugh, 3. (no word needed), 4. patient/kind, 5. popular, 6. spend

Full corrected text (sample):
My uncle Chris is the best uncle I have ever had. He is a gardener, a car mechanic, an electrician and an animal lover all in one. He is unemployed but does a lot of jobs for people. He's very funny. He makes people laugh. He's got lots of ideas. He never gets angry. He's very patient. Many people like him and he is very popular among his family and friends. Uncle Chris loves animals. He can spend hours talking to them, caring about them and doing all sort of jobs. Uncle Chris is 57 tomorrow. "Happy Birthday Uncle Chris"

Detailed Explanation:
1. "does" - Present simple tense for habitual actions. He performs jobs despite being unemployed.
2. "laugh" - Verb that follows "makes people" (make + object + base verb).
3. No word needed - The sentence is complete without an additional word.
4. "patient" or "kind" - Positive adjective describing someone who doesn't get angry.
5. "popular" - Adjective meaning well-liked by many people.
6. "spend" - Verb used with time expressions (spend hours doing something).

2/ Correction:

Answers: 1. live, 2. an, 3. horrible, 4. His, 5. playing, 6. tasty

Full corrected text:
My name is Maria. I live in Valencia in Spain. My mother's name is Aniparo and my father's name is Vicenti. My mother is 40 Years old and my father is 48. My mother is a doctor and my father is an engineer. I love both of them very much. I haven't any sisters, but I've got one horrible brother. His name is Fernando. He goes to the same school as me. He is 8 years old. He enjoys playing football, video games and annoying me! He's a horror! My favorite dish is pizza. It's very tasty.

Detailed Explanation:
1. "live" - Present simple of "to live" (reside). "Leave" means depart, "living" is present participle.
2. "an" - Used before vowel sounds. "Engineer" starts with vowel sound /e/.
3. "horrible" - Fits the context of "He's a horror!" Negative adjective.
4. "His" - Possessive adjective for masculine singular (Fernando). "He's" = he is, "her" is feminine.
5. "playing" - After "enjoys" we use gerund (-ing form).
6. "tasty" - Positive adjective for food. "Disgusting" and "repulsive" are negative.

4/ Correction:

Answers: 1. am staying, 2. am not reading, 3. am sitting, 4. are singing, 5. go, 6. fall, 7. my cousins' jet-ski, 8. noisy

Full corrected text:
Dear Pam,

I am having a fantastic time in Florida, I am staying with my aunt and uncle and two cousins. I have a long list of books to read before next term, but I am not reading any of them at the moment. Right now, I am sitting in the garden with my feet in the swimming pool. The sun is shining and the birds are singing. Every day, we go to the beach. I am learning to water ski, I am not very good yet and I often fall over but it's fun. I sometimes borrow my cousins' jet-ski it's brilliant but it is noisy. I am afraid the people on the beach don't like it.

Please write me back soon

Love
Jane

Detailed Explanation:
1. "am staying" - Present continuous for temporary situations.
2. "am not reading" - Present continuous negative for current activity.
3. "am sitting" - Present continuous for action happening now.
4. "are singing" - Present continuous with plural subject "birds".
5. "go" - Present simple for habitual actions (every day).
6. "fall" - Present simple for habitual actions with "often".
7. "my cousins' jet-ski" - Possessive form (plural cousins + apostrophe).
8. "noisy" - Adjective form (describing the jet-ski). "Noise" is a noun.
