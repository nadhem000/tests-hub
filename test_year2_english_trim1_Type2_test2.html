I. Reading Comprehension - Correction

1. Correction:

Victim's Name Criminal Crime
Francisco Fernandez (and Casey Jordan Marquez) William Atchison School shooting / Murder

Detailed Explanation:
1. Victim's Name: The text mentions two victims: "Francisco Fernandez" and "Casey Jordan Marquez" (paragraph 1).
2. Criminal: "Authorities say 21-year-old William Atchison had planned the attack" (paragraph 2).
3. Crime: The crime is a school shooting/murder as described in paragraph 1: "a gunman... opened fire inside Aztec High School."

2. Correction:

Correct paragraph numbers:

What will school administrators do in remembrance of the victims? 3

A crime took place at a high school 1

Circumstances of the crime 2

Detailed Explanation:
1. Paragraph 3 contains: "School officials plan to transform a space damaged by the shooting into an open-learning area and dedicate it to the victims."
2. Paragraph 1 describes the crime: "a 17-year-old boy killed in a shooting... at a New Mexico high school."
3. Paragraph 2 gives details about how the crime happened: "Authorities say 21-year-old William Atchison had planned the attack..."

3. Correction:

a) Correction:

"a gunman disguised as a student"

Explanation: The text says the gunman was "disguised as a student," meaning he was pretending to be a student but was not actually one. The word "disguised" indicates deception.

b) Correction:

"Fernandez's family remembered him as a teenager who loved sports"

Explanation: The statement says Fernandez "detests" (hates) sports, but the text explicitly states he "loved sports," making the original statement false.

4. Correction:

Correct adjectives: faithful, regretful

Detailed Explanation:
1. Faithful: The school officials are planning to dedicate a space to the victims, showing they are loyal and committed to remembering them.
2. Regretful: Their actions show regret about what happened and a desire to create something positive from the tragedy.
The other adjectives don't fit: "forgetful" is opposite to their actions, "lovely" is not appropriate for officials in this context, and "clumsy" doesn't apply.

5. Correction:

Synonyms:

a- appeared = showed up / gathered

b- the way = path

Detailed Explanation:
1. "showed up" is used in the same paragraph: "showed up to Pinon Hills Community Church." Both "appeared" and "showed up" mean to arrive or become visible.
2. "path" appears in paragraph 2: "A path leading up to the church." "The way" and "path" are synonyms meaning a route or direction.

6. Correction:

Reference words:

a- He refers to the gunman / William Atchison

b- It refers to a space / the space damaged by the shooting

Detailed Explanation:
1. "He" refers to the subject of the previous sentence: "The gunman shot Fernandez..." So "He" = the gunman (William Atchison).
2. "It" refers to the nearest previous noun: "a space damaged by the shooting." The pronoun "it" replaces this noun phrase.

2. Correction:

Sample speech (12 lines):

Respected teachers, distinguished guests, and dear friends,

Today, I stand before you to address a grave issue that plagues our society: child labour.

Child labour refers to the exploitation of children through any form of work that deprives them of their childhood, education, and development.

The effects of this practice are devastating. Physically, children suffer injuries and health problems. Psychologically, they experience trauma and stunted development.

Educationally, they miss out on schooling, perpetuating the cycle of poverty. Economically, it undermines future workforce quality.

To combat this, we need comprehensive solutions. First, strict enforcement of existing laws against child labour is essential.

Second, we must improve access to quality education for all children, especially in rural areas.

Third, poverty alleviation programs should support families so they don't rely on children's income.

Fourth, public awareness campaigns can change societal attitudes toward child labour.

Fifth, consumers should support companies with ethical labor practices.

Every child deserves a childhood filled with learning and play, not work and exploitation.

Let us work together to create a world where no child has to work, and every child can dream. Thank you.
