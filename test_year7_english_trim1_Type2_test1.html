Part I: Language - Correction

1/ Correction:

Answers: 1. are relaxing, 2. washes, 3. is not baking, 4. her, 5. helpful, 6. am telling

Full corrected text:
Today is Saturday. My family and I are relaxing at home. My father usually washes his car on weekdays, but he is reading a book about cars now. My mother usually bakes cookies on weekdays, but she is not baking at the moment. She is watching a cooking show on TV. My older sister usually does her homework, but she is painting some pictures right now. My little brother is a helpful boy. He usually helps my father wash the cars, but he is playing with his toys today. And me? I usually clean the yard, but I am telling you about my family now. What about you?

Detailed Explanation:
1. "are relaxing" - Present continuous tense because it's happening now (Saturday).
2. "washes" - Present simple for habitual actions with "usually".
3. "is not baking" - Present continuous negative for actions happening now.
4. "her" - Possessive adjective for "sister" (feminine).
5. "helpful" - Adjective form of "help" to describe the brother.
6. "am telling" - Present continuous for action happening now.

2/ Correction:

Answers: 1. at, 2. then, 3. finish, 4. active, 5. never, 6. for

Full corrected text:
Sophie is 11 years old and she lives in Brighton, England. She usually gets up at quarter to eight. She has a shower, gets dressed and then she brushes her teeth. After that, she goes downstairs and has breakfast. For breakfast, she usually has milk, toast and orange juice. Then, she goes to the bus stop to catch the bus. Classes start at half past eight and finish at half past four. She usually has lunch at the school canteen at about 12 o'clock. Her best friends, Amy and Sophie, always sit next to her at lunch. After school, she returns home and does her homework. She is an active and a very hard-working pupil and she never misses a school task. At half past seven, it's time for dinner. Then she usually reads a book and chats with her parents for a while. Sophie is a happy little girl!

Detailed Explanation:
1. "at" - We use "at" for specific times (at quarter to eight).
2. "then" - Shows sequence of actions (first shower, then brush teeth).
3. "finish" - Logical opposite of "start" for school schedule.
4. "active" - Positive adjective that matches "hard-working".
5. "never" - Negative adverb that fits with being a good student.
6. "for" - Correct preposition with "it's time for dinner".

3/ Correction:

Answers: 1. same, 2. help, 3. together, 4. After, 5. often, 6. with

Detailed Explanation:
1. "same" - They are in the identical class.
2. "help" - They assist each other with school work.
3. "together" - They play in each other's company.
4. "After" - Time conjunction indicating following school.
5. "often" - Adverb of frequency meaning frequently.
6. "with" - Preposition used with "play with dolls".
Unused words: twice, chat

Dear Peter,

Thank you so much for the beautiful postcard of Norwich! I was very happy to receive it. The picture shows a wonderful city with ancient buildings and a lovely river. Norwich looks like a perfect place to visit with its cathedral and castle. I can see why you're proud of your city.

I live in Jerba, which is an island in southern Tunisia. The weather here is usually warm and sunny, even in December! We have beautiful beaches with white sand and clear blue water. The most famous places in Jerba are Djerba Explore (with crocodiles), the Guellala Museum (about pottery), and the ancient synagogue called Ghriba.

I would like to invite you to visit me in Jerba. You could come during the summer holidays when the weather is perfect for swimming. We could visit all the interesting places together, and you could try our delicious Tunisian food like couscous and brik.

Please write back soon and tell me if you can come. I would be very happy to show you my beautiful island.

Best wishes,
Your friend,
Aly
