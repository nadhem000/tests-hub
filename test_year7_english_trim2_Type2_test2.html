III- Writing (5 marks)

Task : Peter joins his Tunisian friend, Aly Soltane, for a long walk on market day in Jerba. He writes a letter to his sister Jane to describe the market place. Write Peter's letter.

Dear Jane,

Yours, Peter

Assessment: Content /2 ; Lexical appropriacy and grammar /2 ; Spelling and punctuation /1.

Detailed correction

I- Reading Comprehension

1- ✓ b- life on a farm (the whole text describes daily life on a farm).

2- a/ False: They wake up early at around dawn (text: "Every day, they wake up early at around dawn"). b/ False: In the afternoon, Billy and his son go to the outdoor market to sell their produce (text: "After lunch, Billy and his son go to an outdoor market and sell all their fresh vegetables, eggs and milk").

3- "it" refers to the horse called Moonlight.

4- Billy thinks his job is tiring but he is proud of it. He likes it because he says "we are proud of ourselves".

II- Language

Exercise 1: 1. is raining, 2. our, 3. her, 4. is working / works, 5. works, 6. drawing. (Accept "is working" for present continuous, "works" for simple present; both possible).

Exercise 2: 1. clean, 2. middle, 3. are, 4. me, 5. next, 6. ground.

Exercise 3: 1. there, 2. quiet, 3. fresh, 4. onions, 5. them, 6. on, 7. market, 8. sell. (Extra words: quite, in).

III- Writing - Planning and sample letter

Planning (descriptive/narrative letter)

  • Salutation and opening: Greet Jane, mention where you are and why you're writing.
  • Setting the scene: Describe the market day in Jerba – time, weather, atmosphere.
  • Sights and sounds: Describe the stalls, colourful products (spices, fabrics, pottery), people, animals, vendors calling.
  • Smells and tastes: Mention aromas of food, spices, mint tea, trying local delicacies.
  • Activities and feelings: Walking with Aly, bargaining, meeting friendly people, feeling excited and amazed.
  • Closing: Express wish that Jane were there, ask about her, sign off.

Sample letter (approx. 30 lines)

Dear Jane,

I hope you're doing well. I'm writing to you from the beautiful island of Jerba, where I'm spending a few days with my Tunisian friend Aly Soltane. Today we went to the weekly market – it's an incredible experience and I have to tell you all about it!

We woke up early because Aly said the market starts at sunrise. The weather was warm and sunny, with a gentle breeze from the sea. As we walked through the narrow streets of the market, I was amazed by the explosion of colours everywhere. Stalls were piled high with bright oranges, red peppers, shiny olives, and baskets of dates. There were also huge mounds of spices – cumin, coriander, paprika – creating a rainbow of reds, yellows and browns.

The noise was fantastic: vendors shouting out their prices, donkeys braying, chickens clucking in cages, and the constant hum of people bargaining. Aly taught me a few Arabic words to haggle, and I managed to buy a beautiful hand‑painted pottery bowl for just a few dinars!

And the smells! Grilled meat skewers sizzling on charcoal, fresh mint and bread, and the sweet scent of jasmine flowers. We stopped at a small stall and drank steaming mint tea while watching a man make fresh olive oil soap. Aly bought some spicy merguez sausages in a crusty baguette – it was the most delicious thing I've ever tasted.

We walked for hours, weaving through crowds of people in colourful traditional clothes. Some women were wearing beautiful silk scarves, and old men sat in cafes playing cards. Children ran around laughing, chasing each other between the stalls. I felt completely immersed in the vibrant life of the island.

One old carpet seller invited us to sit on his rugs and told us stories about the market's history. He said the same market has been held here for hundreds of years. I bought a small wool rug with traditional Berber patterns – it will look perfect in my room.

As the sun began to set, the market grew quieter and the stallholders started packing up. Aly and I sat by the harbour, watching the fishing boats and eating sweet pastries filled with almonds and honey. I felt so happy and grateful to be here.

I wish you could see all this with your own eyes! I'll bring back lots of photos and some spices for you to try. How are things at home? Write back soon!

Yours,
Peter
