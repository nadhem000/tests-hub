Text: A true story

1/ This is a true story. It happened a few years ago in a small town. All the families in the town watched a lot of TV, but many people were not happy about this. Some people said, 'TV is bad for us. It makes us lazy and unhealthy.' Others said, 'TV is bad for children because there is too much fighting on TV.' Many people said, 'People don't talk when the TV is on, and that's bad for families.'

2/ One person said, 'Let's stop watching TV - just for one month.' A lot of people liked that idea. So 40 families put their TVs in a cupboard and did not watch TV for one month.

3/ Those four weeks, with no TV, were very different. Children played together more than before. Families talked together more and did interesting things together. One father said, 'When we had TV, we did not talk much, but now we enjoy talking and laughing together. That's more interesting and more amusing than TV.' A boy said, 'I've started new hobbies, and I've had a lot of fun with my brother and parents.'

4/ Some fat children lost a few kilos because they spent less time sitting down. They felt fitter and looked better. Others had more time for homework and did better at school. Nearly everybody enjoyed the month with no TV.

5/ So what happened at the end of the month? Did the 40 families throw their TVs away or did they put the TVs back in their living rooms? The answer is surprising. Not one family threw out their TV! Some people watched less than before, but none of them stopped. They all loved that box in the living room too much!

Detailed correction

I- Reading Comprehension

1- Most appropriate title: C/ The advantages and disadvantages of TV (the text presents both positive and negative aspects).

2- Table (possible answers):

  • Before: 1. Children didn't play together much. 2. Families didn't talk much. 3. Fat children sat a lot and didn't lose weight. 4. Less time for homework. (any 3)
  • Now: 1. Children played together more. 2. Families talked and laughed together. 3. Fat children lost weight, felt fitter. 4. More time for homework, better at school. (any 3)

3- a) T – "Some fat children lost a few kilos because they spent less time sitting down." (implies watching TV makes you sit and gain weight). b) T – "Nearly everybody enjoyed the month with no TV." (almost all, not everyone? but "nearly everybody" means most, so true). c) F – "Not one family threw out their TV! Some people watched less than before, but none of them stopped." d) T – "People don't talk when the TV is on."

4- - How many families threw their TV sets away? 0 (none). - How many families stopped watching TV? 0 (none). - How long did they stop watching TV? One month.

5- - "this" refers to the fact that all families watched a lot of TV and many were not happy. - "that box" refers to the television.

III- Writing - Planning and sample paragraph (advantages of TV)

Planning

  • Introduction: TV is a powerful force in our lives; many people enjoy it for several reasons.
  • First advantage: Source of news and information (wars, catastrophes, world events).
  • Second advantage: Increases knowledge about cultures, traditions, and people.
  • Third advantage: Popular and cheap amusement (music, films, sports).
  • Fourth advantage: Relaxation after a tiring day.
  • Conclusion: TV has many benefits, but should be watched in moderation.

Sample paragraph (10 lines)

People like watching television for many reasons. First of all, TV is a major source of news. It keeps us informed about wars, catastrophes, and everything happening around the world. Moreover, it increases our knowledge about different people, their cultures and traditions. We can travel virtually and learn without leaving our homes. Secondly, television is a popular form of amusement. It offers music, films, cartoons, and sports programmes for all ages. Watching TV is comfortable and cheap compared to going out. Finally, whenever we feel tired or bored, we can always turn on the TV and find our favourite programme to relax. So, television has many advantages, but we should not forget to balance it with other activities.

Extra: Narrative Descriptive Article (30 lines) – Example

Planning for narrative (surprise party)

  • Introduction: Set the scene – last Saturday, organised a surprise party for mother.
  • Preparations: Secret planning, shopping, decorating, inviting relatives.
  • During the party: Mother's arrival, surprise, emotions, food, music, speeches.
  • Conclusion: Happy ending, mother's joy, cleanup, feelings.

Narrative Article (30 lines)

Last Saturday was a day I will never forget. My family and I organised a surprise party for my mother's birthday. We had been planning it for weeks, keeping it a secret from her. On the morning of the party, while Mum was out visiting her friend, we sprang into action. My father took charge of the decorations – colourful balloons, streamers, and a big banner that read "Happy Birthday Mum!" My sister prepared a photo slideshow with pictures from Mum's childhood to the present. I went to the supermarket to buy snacks, drinks, and a beautiful chocolate cake. We also invited our close relatives: grandparents, uncles, aunts, and cousins. Everyone promised to arrive by 6 p.m. and hide in the living room.

By 5 p.m., the house was transformed. The table was filled with sandwiches, fruit skewers, and soft drinks. We set up the music playlist and tested the speakers. At 5:45, all the guests arrived quietly and hid behind the sofas and curtains. I was so nervous that my heart was pounding. Finally, at 6 p.m., we heard Mum's key in the door. She walked in, and we all jumped out shouting "SURPRISE!" Her face was priceless – first shock, then tears of joy, and then a huge smile. She hugged each one of us, laughing and crying at the same time.

The party was wonderful. We played her favourite songs and danced in the living room. My uncle made a funny speech that made everyone laugh, and my grandmother shared stories about Mum when she was young. We ate the delicious cake, took hundreds of photos, and even had a small disco with colourful lights. Mum kept saying she couldn't believe we had kept it a secret. The party went on until midnight. At the end, we gave her our gifts – a new scarf from me, a novel from my sister, and a weekend getaway trip from my dad. She was overwhelmed with happiness.

Cleaning up was tiring but we were all so happy. It felt great to see Mum so joyful and appreciated. I'll never forget that evening. It taught me that surprises and family time are priceless.
