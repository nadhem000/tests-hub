II. Language - Correction

1. Correction:

Correct answers:

  1. order - What would you like to order? (Standard restaurant question)
  2. fries - Would you like fries with that? (Common with steak)
  3. please - Yes please. (Polite response)
  4. rings - Onion rings (Common side dish)
  5. drink - What would you like to drink? (Standard beverage question)
Waiter: Good evening Sir, Good evening Madam. What would you like to order?
Waiter: Certainly. Would you like fries with that Sir?
Tom: Yes please. Could I also have a side order of onion rings?
Waiter: No problem. And what would you like to drink?

Detailed Explanation:
1. "order" - In restaurant context, waiters ask "What would you like to order?" to take food requests.
2. "fries" - Steak is commonly served with fries as a side dish in many restaurants.
3. "please" - Polite response to a question offering something. "Yes please" is correct English etiquette.
4. "rings" - "Onion rings" is a specific dish, different from onion salad or other preparations.
5. "drink" - After food orders, waiters ask about beverages with "What would you like to drink?"

Extra words: serve, hungry (not used in this dialogue)

2. Correction:

Correct answers:

  1. her - Karen is female, so "her brother"
  2. rarely - They live in different states, so they rarely see each other
  3. calls - Simple present for habitual action
  4. their - Shared parents, so "their parents"
  5. goodbye - They miss each other, so they don't want to say goodbye

Karen lives in California. She is a bright student. Her brother lives in Ohio. They miss each other. They rarely see each other. Karen calls him every day. They talk about their parents. She also tells him about her day at school. They never want to say goodbye to each other.

Detailed Explanation:
1. "her" - Karen is a girl's name, so possessive pronoun "her" is correct for her brother.
2. "rarely" - Logical choice since they live in different states (California and Ohio) making frequent visits difficult.
3. "calls" - Simple present tense for habitual actions (she calls every day). "Is calling" would be present continuous for action happening now.
4. "their" - They share the same parents, so plural possessive "their" is correct.
5. "goodbye" - Emotional context: they miss each other, so they don't want to part (say goodbye). "Hello" is for greetings.

Detailed Explanation:

3. "Portuguese" - Nationality adjective from Portugal (Portugal ‚Üí Portuguese).
4. "is" - Subject-verb agreement: Amine (singular) + is (singular verb).
5. "are" - There + be construction with plural subject "many passengers".
