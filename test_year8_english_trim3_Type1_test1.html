📜 Listening Transcript (At a Clothing Store)

Scene 1 – In a clothing store. A shopper (woman) is talking to a shop assistant.

Shopper: Excuse me. Yes. I was wondering, do you have any long-sleeved shirts?

Assistant: Yes, we do. Right? Let me check. The small shirts are actually more expensive than the big ones because they are a limited edition. A small shirt costs $45, while a large one is only $35.

Shopper: Oh, I see. And what about pants? I saw your sale sign.

Assistant: We also have specials for sales week. These pants on sale are $29.99. They come in dark colors – navy and black, not light colors.

Shopper: That's fine. I'll take the navy pair.

Scene 2 – The shopper moves to another section.

Shopper: I'm also looking for a pair of boots. But those colorful boots over there – how much are they?

Assistant: They are $59.99.

Shopper: That's not too much more expensive than the sale items. And they are lovely! I'll take them too.

I. LISTENING SKILLS – Detailed Answers & Analysis

I / COMPREHENSION (5 marks):

  • 1° a) boots ✓ – In Scene 2, the shopper explicitly says: "I'm also looking for a pair of boots."
  • 2° Pants on sale: $29.99 – The assistant states: "These pants on sale are $29.99."
  • 3° a) TRUE – Justification: "The small shirts are actually more expensive than the big ones because they are a limited edition."
  • 3° b) FALSE – Justification: "They come in dark colors – navy and black, not light colors." The passage explicitly says the pants are in dark colors, not light ones.
  • 4° lovely – The shopper exclaims at the end of Scene 2: "And they are lovely!" This adjective directly expresses her positive reaction to the colorful boots.

II / FUNCTION (1 mk):

Offering help: "Let me check." / "Yes, we do." / "Right?"

Explanation: The shop assistant's response "Yes, we do. Right? Let me check." constitutes offering help — the assistant acknowledges the customer's request and proactively offers to verify availability. Any of these phrases is acceptable as they demonstrate willingness to assist the shopper.

III / SPELLING (1 mk):

  • long-sleeved – The shopper asks for "long-sleeved shirts" in Scene 1. This compound adjective is hyphenated and describes shirts with full-length sleeves.
  • specials – The assistant says "We also have specials for sales week." This noun (plural) refers to special offers or discounted items during a promotional period.

IV / PRONUNCIATION (1 mk):

  • Right (not "Night") – The assistant says "Right?" as a conversational filler to check understanding. The initial /r/ sound distinguishes it clearly from "night" /naɪt/.
  • cost (not "lost") – In the context "this cost?", the /k/ initial consonant is heard, not /l/. The word "cost" relates to the price inquiry.

Phonetic note: "Right" and "night" form a minimal pair differing only in the initial consonant (/r/ vs /n/). Similarly, "cost" and "lost" differ in the initial consonant (/k/ vs /l/). Careful listening is essential to distinguish these sounds.

II. LANGUAGE – Detailed Answers & Explanations

1° Circle the right alternative (4 mks):

  • more important – Comparative form required because of "than career, money and family." The structure signals comparison between friendships and other life priorities.
  • who – Relative pronoun referring to people ("anyone"). "Which" refers to things; "when" refers to time.
  • true – A "true friend" is a common collocation meaning a genuine, sincere friend. "Fake" is the opposite; "unfaithful" suggests disloyalty.
  • on – "Rely on" is a fixed phrasal verb meaning to depend on or trust someone.
  • stand by me – Means to support someone loyally, especially in difficult times. "Let me down" means disappoint; "laugh at me" means mock.
  • badly – Adverb form needed to modify the verb "behave." "Bad" is an adjective; "worse" is a comparative adjective.
  • after – "Look after" is a phrasal verb meaning to take care of. "Look at" means to observe; "look for" means to search.
  • the dearest – Superlative form used to indicate the closest or most cherished among all friends. The context "one among those" suggests a selection from a group, requiring the superlative.

2° Fill in the blanks (4 mks):

  • (1) touch – "Kept in touch" is a fixed expression meaning maintained contact.
  • (2) humour – "Sense of humour" is a common collocation referring to the ability to find things funny.
  • (3) company – "In his company" means when you are with him; his presence.
  • (4) gets on – "Gets on well with everyone" means has good relationships with everyone.
  • (5) loyal – "Loyal and honest" are parallel adjectives describing Nadia's character. Loyal means faithful and devoted.
  • (6) going – "Easy-going" is a compound adjective describing someone relaxed and tolerant.
  • (7) socialising – "Enjoys socialising" means she likes spending time with people. The gerund follows "enjoy."
  • (8) waiting – "What are you waiting for?" is a rhetorical question encouraging immediate action.

Extra words not used: quarrels (arguments), jealous (envious).

3° Supply the right tense or form (4 mks):

  • lived – Past simple, referring to a completed action before moving to Liverpool in 2012. The time reference "before moving" signals a past event.
  • ourselves – Reflexive pronoun. "We find ourselves returning" means we discover that we are returning. The subject "we" requires the reflexive "ourselves."
  • ten-day – Compound adjective formed from "it lasts ten days." When a duration modifies a noun, it becomes a hyphenated singular form: "a ten-day trip."
  • prepared – Past participle used as an adjective after "be": "be prepared for anything." This is a common fixed expression meaning ready.
  • visitors – Plural noun: people who visit. "Londoners as well as visitors" contrasts residents with tourists.
  • well-dressed – Compound adjective from "good/dress." "Fairly well-dressed" means wearing nice clothes. The adverb "well" modifies the past participle "dressed."
  • uncomfortably – Adverb form modifying the adjective "hot." "Jeans become uncomfortably hot" means so hot that they cause discomfort.
  • will certainly need – Future simple with adverb. The context predicts a future necessity on bright days, and the adverb "certainly" intensifies the prediction.
