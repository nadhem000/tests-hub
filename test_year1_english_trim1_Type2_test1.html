I- Reading Comprehension (15 points)

Reading Passage: The Teacher Who Changed My Life

Page 1:

My name is Nickolas Gage. I was born in a small village in Greece, but my life took a dramatic turn when I was just nine years old. After my mother's tragic death, I boarded a ship alone, traveling across the ocean to join my father who had already settled in the United States. I remember feeling scared and overwhelmed as I left everything familiar behind.

When I arrived at my new school in Massachusetts, I found myself surrounded by classmates who seemed richer, smarter, and better dressed than me. My English was poor, my clothes were different, and I struggled to fit in. I felt like an outsider, constantly aware of the differences between my old life and this new world.

Page 2:

Then I met Miss Hurd, my seventh-grade English teacher. She noticed my interest in stories and encouraged me to join the school newspaper club. But this wasn't just a club where students read newspapers - we actually wrote articles, conducted interviews, and published our own work. Miss Hurd saw something in me that I didn't see in myself.

She spent extra time with me after school, helping me improve my writing. "Everyone has a story to tell," she would say. "And your story matters." Under her guidance, I wrote about my journey from Greece, about losing my mother, about trying to find my place in a new country. For the first time, I felt my experiences had value.

Page 3:

The newspaper club became my sanctuary. Through it, I learned not just how to write, but how to observe, how to question, how to find my voice. Miss Hurd taught me that words have power - they can bridge cultures, heal wounds, and change perspectives.

Years later, as I pursued a career in journalism, I often thought back to those afternoons in the newspaper club. Miss Hurd didn't just teach me English; she taught me how to think critically and express myself clearly. I owe her so much - more than I could ever properly express. Her belief in me transformed a scared immigrant boy into someone who believed he could make a difference through writing.

Today, as I write for major publications, I carry her lessons with me. She showed me that great teachers don't just fill minds with information - they light fires in hearts. And for that, I will always be grateful.

Lexique / Vocabulary

overwhelmed: feeling completely overcome by something

sanctuary: a safe or peaceful place

immigrant: a person who comes to live permanently in a foreign country

perspectives: particular ways of viewing things

critical thinking: the objective analysis of facts to form a judgment
