4. "techniques" - Methods or procedures
5. "applications" - Practical uses of a technology
6. "disorders" - Medical conditions
7. "technique" - Referring to gene therapy method
8. "cures" - Treatments that eliminate disease

The two extra words are "information" and "scientists."

The Digital Trap: How Modern Entertainment is Hijacking Our Youth

By a concerned student

In today's hyper-connected world, modern entertainment has become an inescapable part of young people's lives. While technology offers incredible opportunities, its dark side is increasingly evident as addiction to video games, social media, and streaming platforms takes a heavy toll on our generation.

The most alarming effect is on behavior. Excessive screen time leads to irritability, aggression, and social withdrawal. The instant gratification of digital entertainment reduces attention spans and patience for real-world activities. Many young gamers exhibit symptoms similar to substance addiction, including mood swings and neglect of personal hygiene.

Relationships suffer profoundly. Face-to-face interactions are replaced by virtual connections, weakening social skills and empathy. Family dinners are disrupted by phone notifications, and meaningful conversations give way to superficial online chats. This digital isolation creates a generation that knows how to accumulate online friends but struggles with genuine intimacy.

Academic performance inevitably declines. Homework is neglected in favor of one more game level or episode. Sleep deprivation from late-night browsing affects concentration and memory. The constant distraction of notifications makes sustained study nearly impossible, leading to poor grades and limited future opportunities.

Physical health deteriorates too. Sedentary lifestyles contribute to obesity, while screen glare damages eyesight. The blue light from devices disrupts sleep cycles, creating a vicious cycle of fatigue and dependence on caffeine or energy drinks.

To my fellow students, I urge you to recognize these dangers. Set strict time limits for entertainment devices. Prioritize real relationships over virtual ones. Remember that your future depends on the choices you make today. Let's not allow digital entertainment to control our lives when we should be controlling it.

The technology itself isn't evil‚Äîit's our relationship with it that needs examination. By cultivating balance and self-discipline, we can enjoy modern entertainment without letting it define us. Our youth is too precious to spend entirely in a digital bubble.
