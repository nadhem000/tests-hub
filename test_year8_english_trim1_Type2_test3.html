I. Reading Comprehension - Correction

1. Correction:

Student Subject Reason to go to school
Dad Computer Science He wants a better job / To get a degree for a better job

Detailed Explanation:
The text explicitly states in paragraph 1: "He goes to school at the community college where he is studying computer science." This answers the "Subject" column. For the "Reason to go to school," paragraph 2 explains: "Dad says he is studying hard because he wants a better job. He says he's looking forward to being home in the evenings and relaxing with all of us. In another year he will finish his degree and he can find a better job." The primary reason is clearly to get a better job by completing his degree.

2. Correction:

Dad encourages his son by patting him on the shoulder and saying, "You're doing very well. You keep it up and you won't have to go to school when you're an old guy like me."

Detailed Explanation:
The text states in the final paragraph: "Sometimes when I'm not in the mood to do my homework he pats me on the shoulder and says, 'You're doing very well. You keep it up and you won't have to go to school when you're an old guy like me.'" This direct quote shows exactly how Dad encourages his son - through physical affection (patting on the shoulder) and encouraging words that emphasize the benefits of studying hard now to avoid difficulties later in life.

3. Correction:

state = grade

Detailed Explanation:
In paragraph 1, the text says: "My dad isn't in a grade." The word "grade" in this context means a level or stage in an educational system, which is synonymous with "state" when referring to educational standing. In American English, "grade" specifically refers to a year of school (first grade, second grade, etc.), which corresponds to the meaning of "state" as a level or position.

4. Correction:

"then he says goodbye to us" (paragraph 1): the narrator and his sister

Detailed Explanation:
The pronoun "us" refers to the narrator and his sister. Earlier in the paragraph, it states: "Every morning he walks with my sister and me to the bus stop. We all ride the same bus to my school and then he says goodbye to us." The "us" clearly includes both children who are going to school together. In English, pronouns must refer back to previously mentioned nouns, and in this context, "my sister and me" are the only people Dad would be saying goodbye to at the school.

5. Correction:

c- Opinion

Detailed Explanation:
The phrase "I think" clearly indicates that what follows is the narrator's personal opinion rather than a fact. An opinion expresses someone's beliefs, feelings, or thoughts about something, while contrast shows differences between things, and request asks for something. The statement "I think Dad has the most homework" is the narrator's personal assessment based on observation, not a contrast between different things or a request for action.

1. Correction:

1. However (shows contrast between his intention and reality)
2. at (phrasal verb "look at" meaning to check or observe)
3. worried (emotional state after missing his flight)
4. beautifully (adverb modifying the verb "danced")
5. took (past simple for completed action in the past)

Detailed Explanation:
1. "However" is used to show contrast between what he intended (catching the right bus) and what actually happened (the bus was for a party).
2. "look at" is the correct phrasal verb meaning to direct one's gaze toward something, in this case, his watch.
3. "worried" describes his emotional state after realizing he missed his flight.
4. "beautifully" is the adverb form needed to modify the verb "danced" (how he danced).
5. "took" is the past simple tense used for a completed action that happened at a specific time in the past (the next morning).

2. Correction:

2. student (noun form from "study")
3. success (noun form from "succeed")

Detailed Explanation:
1. "will get" is future simple tense used for future plans or predictions about what degree you will obtain.
2. "student" is the noun form meaning someone who studies at a school.
3. "success" is the noun form needed as the subject of the sentence (your success).
4. "will be" is future simple tense for a future state or condition.
5. "immediately" is the adverb form meaning without delay, modifying the phrase "after high school."
