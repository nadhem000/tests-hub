I. Reading Comprehension - Correction

1. Correction:

b. Making an announcement on the school over the loud speakers

Detailed Explanation:
The passage begins with "Good morning everyone. This is Principal Smith and I would like to have your attention for a few moments." This opening suggests a spoken announcement, not a written document. The principal directly addresses teachers and students, asking teachers to ensure students are attentive, which is typical of a public address system announcement in a school setting. The tone is conversational and immediate, characteristic of a live announcement rather than a blog post or letter.

2. Correction:

Kensington West High School Skipping non-required classes Punishment
Graduating seniors Exceeding 15% absence rate Required to attend summer school

Detailed Explanation:
In paragraph 2, the principal states: "if you are a graduating senior and you exceed a 15% absence rate, you will be required to attend summer school to make up for the missed classes." The table asks for the specific group affected (graduating seniors), the action (exceeding 15% absence rate), and the consequence (required to attend summer school). This is a clear cause-and-effect relationship outlined in the announcement.

3. Correction:

The principal no longer feels disappointed because the school debate team has taken top prize among other schools of their size and Mr. Jacobs has been nominated for the Excellence in Teaching award.

Detailed Explanation:
In paragraph 4, the principal explicitly states: "But I don't want to speak to you only about negative issues, so here are a couple of things that I am extremely proud to tell you about." He then mentions two achievements: 1) the school debate team winning top prize, and 2) Mr. Jacobs being nominated for a teaching award. These positive accomplishments counterbalance the earlier negative issues (skipping classes and littering) and give the principal reason to be proud rather than disappointed.

4. Correction:

The school Caretaker / the custodian = janitor

Detailed Explanation:
In paragraph 3, the text reads: "The janitor has informed me that he is picking up all kinds of trash..." The word "janitor" is a synonym for school caretaker or custodian - the person responsible for cleaning and maintaining the school building. This is a straightforward vocabulary question where the answer is directly stated in the text.

5. Correction:

ours: the school belongs to all of us (the students, teachers, and principal)

Detailed Explanation:
In paragraph 3, the principal says: "This school is not only his! It's ours!" The word "ours" is a possessive pronoun referring to shared ownership. The context shows the principal is emphasizing collective responsibility - the school belongs to everyone in the school community (students, teachers, staff), not just the janitor. This is a call for shared responsibility in maintaining cleanliness.
