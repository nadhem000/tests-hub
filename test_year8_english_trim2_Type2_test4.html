III- Writing (6 marks)

Task : Last Saturday, you celebrated your 14th birthday. Write an e-mail to your friend to tell him / her about the preparations before the party and what you did during the party (describe the atmosphere).

To: friend@example.com
Subject: My 14th birthday party

Assessment: Adherence to task and content adequacy /2 ; Lexical appropriacy and grammar accuracy /2 ; Mechanical accuracy /2.

Detailed correction

I- Reading Comprehension

1- Spare time activities (the text describes Mary's free time activity: going to cinema).

2- Activity: going to the cinema / watching a film; Date: Saturday afternoon; Company: her school-friends.

3- a) The film is called Percy. b) Percy's wife (or "his wife") called the police.

4- Yes, they liked the movie. Justification: "Mary and her friends laughed a lot." (last sentence).

II- Language

Exercise 1: decided, were playing, were reading (or "played" / "read" but past continuous is better for background). Accept "played" and "read" (simple past) as they describe simultaneous actions. We'll keep "were playing", "were reading".

Exercise 2: spare, hang, fun, chat, looks. (Extra words: funny, are)

Exercise 3: how far, 1 hour, why, there.

Exercise 4: 1→c (ability in the future), 2→d (giving opinion), 3→a (giving advice), 4→b (expressing agreement). (Extra function: e describing)

III- Writing - Planning and example

Planning (narrative descriptive article)

  • Introduction (greeting, reason for writing): Greet friend, say you're writing to tell about your 14th birthday party last Saturday.
  • Preparations (what we did before): Planning with family, sending invitations, decorating the house, buying food, cake, balloons, choosing music, preparing games.
  • During the party (atmosphere, feelings): Arrival of guests, music, dancing, fun games, birthday cake with candles, opening presents, laughter, happy moments.
  • Conclusion (how it ended, feelings now): Party ended late, cleaning up, feeling happy and grateful, looking forward to next celebration.

Sample email (30 lines)

To: friend@example.com
Subject: My 14th birthday party

Dear Sarah,

How are you? I hope you're doing well. I'm writing to tell you all about my 14th birthday party that I celebrated last Saturday. It was absolutely amazing and I can't wait to share every detail with you!

The preparations started a week before. My mum and I sat down and made a list of friends to invite. I designed colourful invitations and handed them out at school. We decided to have a theme: "Hollywood Glam". My dad helped me hang gold and silver streamers all over the living room. We also blew up lots of balloons and put up a big "Happy 14th Birthday" banner. On Saturday morning, my older sister baked a delicious chocolate cake with strawberry frosting. I decorated it with edible glitter and fourteen candles. We prepared snack platters with mini sandwiches, cheese sticks, and fruit skewers. My brother set up a playlist with my favourite pop songs. By 4 p.m., everything was ready and I was so excited I could hardly stand still!

Guests started arriving at 5 p.m. The doorbell kept ringing and soon the house was filled with my closest friends. Everyone loved the decorations. We danced in the living room, played musical chairs and had a karaoke contest. The atmosphere was electric – lots of laughter, singing and cheering. At 7 p.m., my family brought out the cake with all the candles lit. Everyone sang "Happy Birthday" and I made a wish before blowing out the candles. I felt so happy and loved. Then came the best part: opening presents! I got some wonderful gifts – a new sketchbook, a cool backpack, and a book I'd been wanting for ages.

The party went on until 10 p.m. We took loads of photos and selfies. When the last guest left, I helped my parents tidy up. I was tired but incredibly happy. It was the best birthday ever! I'm so grateful to my family and friends for making it so special.

Anyway, I'd love to hear your news. Write back soon!

Love,
Emma
