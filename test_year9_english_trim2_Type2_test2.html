III- Writing – Planning and sample talk (argumentative)

Planning (structure of the talk)

  • Introduction: Greet schoolmates, state the purpose – address the issue of internet addiction.
  • Body – effects:
    • Physical health: eye strain, headaches, sedentary lifestyle, lack of sleep.
    • Mental health: anxiety, depression, comparison on social media.
    • Social life: isolation, less face‑to‑face interaction, weakened relationships.
    • School results: lower concentration, poor grades, procrastination.
  • Conclusion: Encourage reducing screen time, suggest alternatives (sports, hobbies, meeting friends), offer support.

Sample talk (approx. 30 lines)

Dear schoolmates,

Good morning! Thank you for coming today. I’ve gathered you here because I’m worried about something that affects many of us: internet addiction. We spend hours online – before class, during breaks, and after school – often without realising how it harms us.

First, think about your physical health. Staring at screens for too long causes eye strain, headaches, and poor posture. Many of us skip sports or outdoor activities, becoming sedentary. And when we scroll late at night, we lose sleep, which leaves us exhausted the next day.

Our mental health suffers too. Constant comparison with others on social media can lead to anxiety and low self‑esteem. We feel lonely even when we’re connected. Some studies even link heavy internet use to depression.

What about our social life? Instead of talking face to face, we message each other from our rooms. We miss out on real friendships, shared laughter, and the warmth of being together. Our relationships become shallow.

And our school results? We procrastinate, check notifications while studying, and find it hard to concentrate. Grades drop, and we feel stressed trying to catch up.

But it’s not too late. We can take small steps: limit screen time, turn off notifications during study hours, and pick up a hobby – football, reading, painting. Meet friends in the park instead of online. Let’s support each other to break free from the screen.

Remember, the internet is a tool, not a life. Let’s use it wisely and live fully. Thank you.
