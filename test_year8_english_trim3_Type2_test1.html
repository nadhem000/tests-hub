THE TEXT

To: sarahwong@gmail.com
From: peterchan@gmail.com
Subject: Let's save the Earth

Hi Sarah,

(1) I didn't see you at school yesterday. Are you sick? I hope that you are ok. You missed our lesson with Mr Peters. He taught us all about pollution. I feel very upset because many people do not care about this serious problem.

(2) Did you know that there is an invisible, protective layer of gas around the Earth called the ozone layer? The ozone layer makes sure that the temperature on Earth is not too hot or too cold.

(3) But Mr Peters told us that if humans continue to pollute the air, holes will develop in the ozone layer. This would mean that the sun could shine too strongly through the holes and cause the Earth's temperature to rise. If this happens, the ice in the colder areas of the world would start to melt. If too much ice melts, then this water enters our seas and oceans and the water level would rise. This would cause terrible flooding all over the world.

(4) What can we do to prevent this? Mr Peters says we should come up with ideas to help solve this problem and he wants us to design a poster for homework. Have you got any ideas?

See you on Monday.

From,
Peter

III- Writing (6 marks)

Your online friend, Chris, feels bored and sometimes gets in trouble because his parents leave him alone at home and go to work. He hasn't got any friends to keep him company.

Send an e-mail to advise him to strike up new friendships.

Write about 10 to 15 lines. Pay attention to the e-mail format (subject line, greeting, body, closing).

Detailed Correction

I- Reading Comprehension (6 marks)

1/ The text is: b- an e-mail. Peter told Sarah about: a- a lesson which he attended at school. (2 marks)

2/ Peter feels upset because many people do not care about the serious problem of pollution. (1 mark)

3/ this (paragraph 4) refers to the problem of pollution / holes developing in the ozone layer / the environmental crisis described in paragraph 3. (1 mark)

4/ Cause: Humans continue to pollute the air. (1 mark)

5/ (Open answer – sample) I would suggest using public transport instead of cars, planting more trees, reducing the use of harmful chemicals, and raising awareness about the importance of protecting the ozone layer. (1 mark)

II- Language (8 marks)

1/ Verb/word forms (2.5 marks):

1- older   2- arguing   3- felt   4- herself   5- best


2/ Fill in the blanks (3 marks):

1- collect   2- destroying   3- of   4- aware   5- sensitize   6- their

Extra words: waste, theirs (and the second of).


3/ Circle the right option (2.5 marks):

1- nerves   2- into   3- upset   4- himself   5- guilty

III- Writing (6 marks)

Sample e-mail (about 14 lines):

Subject: Making new friends – you're not alone!

Hi Chris,

I was really sorry to hear that you feel bored and sometimes get in trouble at home. I understand that it's hard when your parents work a lot and you're on your own. But don't worry – things can get better!

Here are some ideas. First, why don't you join a sports club or an after‑school activity? It's a brilliant way to meet new people who enjoy the same things as you. You could also try talking to classmates you don't know very well yet – maybe invite someone to play video games or study together.

Another thing you could do is talk to your parents and explain how lonely you feel. They probably don't realise what you're going through. Maybe they could arrange for you to spend time with cousins or family friends after school.

Remember, making friends takes a little courage and patience, but it's worth it. Be yourself, stay positive, and don't give up. Good friendships will come.

Write back and tell me how it goes!

Your friend,
[Your name]


Marking criteria: Relevance to topic (2 marks) – E‑mail format (1 mark) – Coherence & organisation (1.5 marks) – Grammar & spelling (1.5 marks).
