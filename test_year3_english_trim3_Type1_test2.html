I. LANGUAGE – Detailed Answers & Explanations

1. Fill in the blanks (4 marks):

  • impact – "major impact" is a common collocation meaning strong effect.
  • dramatically – adverb needed to describe how life standards changed.
  • spend – "spend time" is the correct verb.
  • less – opposite of "more" when giving reduced attention to family.
  • increased – past participle adjective or verb; computers have made plagiarism rise.
  • over – "accessible over the internet" means via the internet.
  • which – relative pronoun referring to "disadvantages".

Extra word: instead (not used).

2. Multiple choice (4 marks):

  • use – noun required after "the".
  • and – positive addition: "involves ... and does not include".
  • these – plural demonstrative referring to "recombinant DNA techniques".
  • genetically – adverb modifying "engineered".
  • most – superlative form of "common".
  • an – before vowel sound "unspecified".
  • accomplished – means achieved/done; fits the context.
  • into – preposition of movement: insert into the host.

3. Word form (4 marks):

  • biggest – superlative adjective ("one of the biggest").
  • provided – past participle in reduced relative clause: "method (which is) provided".
  • their – possessive adjective.
  • personalizing – gerund after "no matter on ...".
  • are released – passive present simple for habitual action.
  • get – infinitive after "in order to".
  • habits – plural noun.
  • to spend – infinitive of purpose after "causing them".

2. Essay (6 marks) – Sample for topic (a) "Advantages and disadvantages of television":

Television: A Double-Edged Sword

Television has become an integral part of modern life, offering both significant benefits and notable drawbacks. On the positive side, television is a powerful educational tool. Channels like National Geographic and The History Channel provide informative content for all ages. Moreover, television brings world events directly into our living rooms, allowing us to witness history as it unfolds. It can also teach children important values through carefully designed programs.

However, television is not without its disadvantages. The proliferation of channels means children are increasingly exposed to inappropriate content intended for mature audiences. Even with parental controls, it is difficult to completely shield young viewers from violence or unsuitable material. Additionally, excessive television viewing can lead to a sedentary lifestyle and reduce time spent on homework or physical activity.

In conclusion, while television is a valuable source of education and entertainment, it must be used responsibly. Parents should monitor what their children watch and encourage a balanced approach that includes reading, sports, and family interaction. When used wisely, television can enrich our lives rather than detract from them.

Scoring guidelines: Content & organization (2 marks), Vocabulary & grammar (2 marks), Mechanics (punctuation, spelling) (1 mark), Adherence to topic (1 mark).

Similar sample essays for topics (b) and (c) would follow the same structure: introduction, body paragraphs with advantages/impacts, and a balanced conclusion.
