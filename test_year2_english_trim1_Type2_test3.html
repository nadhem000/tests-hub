(1) Marilaine was one of 200,000 or more Haitian children called restaveks, typically serving as unpaid maids in strangers' homes, working for room and board. It is a vast system of child trafficking that is often characterized as a modern form of slavery.

(2) Marilaine grew up in a remote village where no family planning or public schooling is available, one of 12 children to impoverished parents who later separated. As Marilaine tells the story, one day when she was 10 years old, she walked to her father's house to ask him to help pay her school fees. Instead, he dispatched her here to the capital to work as a restavek, a Creole term used to describe child labourers, without even telling her mother.

(3) As is common for restaveks, Marilaine slept on the floor and woke up at 5 each morning to clean the house, fetch water and wash dishes. She says she was beaten daily with electrical cords. Marilaine was allowed no contact with her family. Once, she says, she tried to run away but was caught and beaten.

(4) An aid group called the Restavek Freedom Foundation helped Marilaine escape her home and find refuge in a safe house for restaveks. The mood was festive in the beautiful home as the dozen girls living there cheered Marilaine's arrival and hugged her. Marilaine picked up a book, telling me that she wasn't allowed to touch books at her old house. She tried on new clothes. She slept in a bed.

(5) One way to fight such human trafficking would be to provide free and accessible birth control, so that women like Marilaine's mother don't end up with 12 children that they struggle to feed. Another would be to provide free public education, so that parents don't feel that the only way to get schooling for their children is to send them off as restaveks.

While options b and c describe aspects of the situation, they don't capture the exploitative nature or the forced aspect that option a does. 3. Correction:

a) False statement correction:

"serving as unpaid maids" OR "unpaid maids"

Explanation: The statement claims children get a salary, but the text explicitly says they are "unpaid maids." The word "unpaid" directly contradicts the idea of receiving a salary. Any sentence from paragraph 1 containing "unpaid" would prove the statement false.

b) False statement correction:

"she walked to her father's house to ask him to help pay her school fees"

Explanation: The statement says she failed at school, but the text shows she was trying to continue her education by asking for help with school fees. This indicates she was attending school and wanted to continue, not that she had failed.

c) False statement correction:

"Marilaine was allowed no contact with her family"

Explanation: The statement claims she visited her family, but the text explicitly states she was "allowed no contact with her family." This directly contradicts the idea of regular visits.

d) False statement correction:

"An aid group called the Restavek Freedom Foundation helped Marilaine escape"

Explanation: The statement says girls in the Foundation helped her run away, but the text says an aid group (the Foundation) helped her. The girls in the safe house cheered her arrival after she was rescued, but they didn't help her escape.
