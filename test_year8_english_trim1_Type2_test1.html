Creating a Better School Environment for Everyone

After reading the recent post about improving our school, I've been thinking about what changes could make our school a better place where students can learn without stress and achieve better results. Here are my suggestions:

First, I believe we need more green spaces in our school. Research shows that spending time in nature reduces stress and improves concentration. We could create a small garden in the courtyard where students can relax during breaks or even have outdoor classes on nice days.

Second, I suggest implementing a "quiet zone" in the library where students can study without distractions. Many students struggle to find peaceful places to concentrate, especially during exam periods. This would help everyone focus better on their studies.

Third, I think we should have more flexible seating options in classrooms. Traditional desks and chairs aren't comfortable for everyone. Adding some comfortable chairs, bean bags, or standing desks would allow students to choose what works best for their learning style.

Finally, I propose creating a peer tutoring program where older students can help younger ones with difficult subjects. This would not only improve academic results but also build a stronger sense of community in our school.

These changes could make our school a more enjoyable and productive place for everyone. What do you think about these ideas?
