Exercise 2: 1. at, 2. full, 3. food, 4. arms, 5. stretch, 6. them. (Extra words: invite, drinks).

Exercise 3: 1→e (inviting), 2→d (declining), 3→a (arranging a meeting), 4→b (accepting? but "that would be nice to" indicates acceptance, then 5→f (expressing doubt). III- Writing - Planning and example

Planning (narrative descriptive article)

  • Introduction (greeting, reason for writing): Say hi to Peter, explain that you're writing to tell about the surprise party for mum.
  • Preparations (what we did before): Planning secretly, buying decorations, cake, gifts, inviting relatives, preparing food, cleaning the house.
  • During the party (atmosphere, feelings): Mum's arrival, surprise, emotions, music, dancing, laughter, food, speeches, gifts.
  • Conclusion (how it ended, feelings now): Mum's happiness, everyone enjoyed, late night, cleaning up, feeling proud.

Sample email (30 lines)

To: Peter.smith@yahoo.uk
Subject: Surprise party for my mother

Dear Peter,

How are you? I hope you're having a great week. I'm writing to tell you about the amazing surprise party we organized for my mother last Saturday. It was a huge success and I can't wait to share every detail with you!

For weeks, my father, my sister and I planned everything in secret. We created a private group chat to discuss ideas without Mum finding out. We decided to decorate the living room with balloons, streamers, and a big banner that said "Happy Birthday Mum!" I went shopping with my aunt to buy a beautiful cake – it was chocolate with fresh strawberries, her favourite. We also prepared a photo slideshow with pictures from her childhood to the present.

On Saturday morning, while Mum was out with her friend for coffee, we rushed to set everything up. My dad took care of the music playlist, my sister arranged the flowers, and I prepared the snacks: mini sandwiches, fruit skewers, and soft drinks. By 5 p.m., the house looked like a party hall. We invited our close relatives – grandparents, uncles, aunts, and cousins – and everyone hid behind the couches waiting for the surprise.

When Mum walked in at 6 p.m., we all jumped out and shouted "SURPRISE!" Her face was priceless – she was so shocked that she started crying tears of joy. Then she laughed and hugged every one of us. The atmosphere was absolutely wonderful. We played her favourite songs and danced in the living room. My uncle made a funny speech that made everyone laugh, and my grandmother shared stories about Mum when she was young.

We ate the delicious cake, took hundreds of photos, and even had a small disco with colourful lights. Mum kept saying she couldn't believe we had kept it a secret. The party went on until midnight. At the end, we gave her our gifts – a new scarf from me, a novel from my sister, and a weekend getaway trip from my dad. She was overwhelmed with happiness.

Cleaning up was tiring but we were all so happy. It felt great to see Mum so joyful and appreciated. I'll never forget that evening. Anyway, I'd love to hear your news. Write back soon!

Best wishes,
[Your name]
